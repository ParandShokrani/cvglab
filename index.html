Taryn Clarke

Taryn Clarke is a doctoral researcher with a strong interest in mechanobiology and translational medicine. She holds an Honours Bachelor’s degree in Biomedical Engineering, during which she gained 8 months of industry experience in the Research & Development sector at Johnson & Johnson Medtech Neurovascular. This experience provided her with valuable exposure to the medical device industry and the design of neurovascular solution technologies. She is currently pursuing a PhD in Biomedical Engineering under the TrapKill Project where cutting edge solutions for glioblastoma treatment are being explored through the use of functionalized hydrogels. Motivated by a passion for advancing technologies at the intersection of oncology and neuroengineering, Taryn is driven by the potential of research to meaningfully impact the future of science and medicine.

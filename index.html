José Miguel Fernández Real

José Miguel Fernández Real is a doctoral researcher in tissue engineering and oncology. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering from the Universidad de Málaga, where he completed his thesis on nanotechnology-based strategies for treating osseous cancer. During his undergraduate studies, he also gained hands-on experience in tissue engineering, 3D modelling, and programming, building a versatile skill set at the intersection of biology, engineering, and computation. He later earned a Master's in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Galway, further deepening his expertise in advanced biomedical technologies and translational research. José Miguel is pursuing a PhD in Biomedical Engineering under the TrapKill Project, which explores cutting-edge therapies for glioblastoma by developing functionalized hydrogels. His work integrates biomaterials science with oncology to advance innovative treatment strategies for aggressive brain tumours. As a Spanish researcher passionate about scientific innovation, José Miguel is motivated by the potential of biomedical research to impact the future of oncology and neuroscience.

Our Research

Our work focuses on modifying spatial, temporal, and mechanical signals in healthy and diseased brain cells to influence cell fate. These changes enable us to explore and develop two key areas: 

  • Tthree-dimensional (3D) in vitro culture platforms for studying neurological diseases.

    • Understanding the Glycan Modulation in Gliosis after Traumatic Brain Injury (PhD project, Akash Garhwal)

  • the identification of potential markers for the design and functionalization of biomaterials, especially for neuro-oncology therapies.

    • Key topics of interest include:

    Gliosis, astrocytes, 3D in vitro models, organoids, assembloids, cell-cell interactions, the extracellular matrix (ECM), syndecans, cytoskeletal organization, nucleoskeletal organization, brain tumors, and brain mechanobiology.
